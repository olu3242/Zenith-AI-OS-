AI GOVERNANCE &

Most AI products score 0 in Domain 13. It's the gap between "we ship AI features" and "we ship AI responsibly." The five controls below define what responsible means operationally.

CONTROL 13.1

GOVERNANCE POLICY

A public, versioned AI governance policy document linked from the product. Not a blog post — a structured policy with scope, principles, and update cadence.

EU AI Act · Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 · In force Aug 2024

The EU AI Act is the world's first comprehensive AI law. It classifies AI systems by risk level — Unacceptable, High, Limited, Minimal — and imposes conformity assessments, transparency obligations, and human oversight mandates for high-risk systems. Non-compliance carries fines up to €35M or 7% of global annual turnover.

UK AI Regulation Framework · Pro-innovation Approach 2023

The UK takes a principles-based, sector-specific approach. Five cross-sector principles apply to all AI: Safety & Security, Transparency & Explainability, Fairness, Accountability & Governance, and Contestability & Redress. Unlike the EU, there is no single binding AI law — sector regulators (FCA, ICO, CQC) apply the principles within their remit.

FAQ

COMMON QUESTIONS.

Everything you need to know about the audit, the framework, and regional compliance.

Is this really free? What's the catch? +
Yes, completely free. No credit card, no hidden tier. We offer the free audit because the gap register reveals remediation work that many organisations need help executing — that's our commercial service. The audit itself will never be paywalled.
What kind of AI products does this framework apply to? +
AIOS-STANDARD applies to any production AI system: SaaS platforms with AI features, standalone AI agents, LLM-powered APIs, internal automation tools, and AI OS platforms. It covers both simple AI-enabled apps (L1) and complex multi-agent platforms (L4–L5). If your product makes LLM calls in production, this framework applies.
How long does the audit take to complete? +
The submission form takes 5 minutes. We then review your product against all 65 controls and return the full report within 48 hours. For complex enterprise products we may request a brief technical walkthrough — this adds up to 24 hours to the turnaround.
What does the report actually look like? +
You receive a structured PDF and an interactive dashboard. The PDF contains your overall score, 13 domain scores, certification level, full gap register (every gap with severity and remediation), lapse register, risk register, and a 30/60/90-day roadmap. If you selected a region, it includes a regional compliance score and mandatory control status.
How is the overall score calculated? +
Each of the 65 controls is scored 0–5. Domain scores are calculated as (sum of scores / max possible) × 100. The overall score is a weighted average across all 13 domains, where Domain 13 (Governance) carries 12%, Security & Agent Orchestration carry 9% each, and most others carry 7–8%.
Why does Domain 13 (Governance) carry the highest weight? +
Governance failure creates legal, reputational, and regulatory risk that compounds every other gap. A technically excellent product that ships without a governance policy, bias testing, or human oversight mechanisms will fail enterprise procurement, regulatory audit, and — increasingly — legal review. We weight it highest because the consequences of failure are highest.
What does a zero in Policy Engine actually cost me? +
Domain 07 (Policy Engine) has an 8% weight. A zero score in that domain costs you 8 points on your overall score. But the bigger cost is qualitative: a Policy Engine score of zero will be classified as a CRITICAL gap in your gap register and will block L3 certification, since enterprise readiness requires access control and content policy to be in place.
How does the regional compliance multiplier work? +
When you select a region, we evaluate your product against that region's mandatory controls. Your pass rate determines a compliance multiplier between 0.85 and 1.0. Your final score equals base score × multiplier. If you pass all mandatory controls, the multiplier is 1.0 and your score is unchanged. The multiplier never drops below 0.85 regardless of how many controls you fail — the floor prevents artificially catastrophic scores.
What exactly is a "governance policy document"? +
Control 13.1 requires a public, versioned, structured policy document — not a marketing page about responsible AI. It must include: scope of AI use, core principles, prohibited uses, human oversight mechanisms, update cadence, and a named responsible party. It should be linkable and discoverable from your product or website. A blog post does not qualify.
We're a small team. Do we really need an ethics review process? +
Control 13.5 scales with team size. For teams under 10, a structured review checklist completed before each major AI capability ships is sufficient for a score of 3/5. A dedicated ethics board is only required for 5/5. The gap register will show you the minimum viable version for your current certification target — you don't need to over-engineer this at L2 or early L3.
What's the difference between human oversight (13.3) and a policy engine (07)? +
Domain 07 (Policy Engine) is about automated access control — rate limits, content filters, tool approval gates, and usage policies enforced in code. Control 13.3 (Human Oversight) is about human-in-the-loop for high-risk AI decisions — a mechanism for a human to review, override, or escalate before a consequential decision is enacted. You need both.
What does bias & fairness testing (13.2) look like in practice? +
At minimum (score 2/5), it's a documented manual review process before major model changes. For score 4/5 (required for L4), it's automated evaluation integrated into your CI/CD pipeline — running fairness metrics on a test set and failing the build if demographic parity thresholds are breached. Tools like Fairlearn or AI Fairness 360 provide the evaluation primitives; you need to write the integration and define your thresholds.
Do I need to comply with the EU AI Act if I'm not based in Europe? +
The EU AI Act has extraterritorial reach — it applies to any AI system placed on the EU market or used by EU users, regardless of where the provider is based. This is analogous to GDPR. If any of your users are in the EU, you are within scope. We strongly recommend selecting EU as your region if you have any EU presence, even indirect. This is not legal advice — consult qualified EU law counsel for your specific situation.
What's the difference between a "mandatory" and "recommended" regional control? +
Mandatory controls affect your compliance multiplier — failing them reduces your final score. They represent controls where the relevant law explicitly requires the capability (e.g., EU AI Act Art. 14 for human oversight). Recommended controls are best practices under the framework but are not yet explicit legal requirements. They don't affect the multiplier but will appear as gaps in your gap register.
We operate in multiple regions. How does scoring work? +
When you operate across multiple jurisdictions, we apply the most stringent applicable overlay as your primary compliance multiplier. Your report will also include a side-by-side comparison of all applicable regional requirements so you can identify controls that satisfy multiple frameworks simultaneously. In practice, ISO 42001 alignment plus EU AI Act mandatory controls covers most of the requirements in US, UK, and Canadian frameworks as well.
The Canada AIDA isn't in force yet. Should I include it? +
AIDA is part of Bill C-27, which passed second reading and is expected to receive royal assent. We recommend including it if you operate in Canada — not because it's legally required today, but because enterprise procurement teams are already asking about it, and the remediation lead time for high-impact system designation and risk assessment is typically 6–12 months. Getting ahead of it now is significantly cheaper than scrambling when it passes.
Is AIOS certification legally recognised? +
AIOS-STANDARD is an independent framework, not a regulatory certification. It is not formally recognised by the EU, UK, or any regulator in lieu of actual compliance. What it does provide is: structured evidence of AI maturity that enterprise procurement teams can evaluate, a gap register that maps directly to regulatory requirements, and a roadmap toward frameworks like ISO 42001 that are formally certifiable. Think of it as the audit that tells you what you need to do to get the certifications that matter legally.
What's the fastest path from L2 to L3? +
L3 requires a score of 55+ and Domain 13 ≥ 40. Most L2 products can reach L3 in a 60-day sprint by: (1) shipping a governance policy document (13.1) and establishing a human oversight mechanism (13.3), (2) building out a policy engine with basic content filtering and rate limits (Domain 07), and (3) adding observability traces for agent execution (Domain 10). Your gap register will show you the exact controls with the highest score-per-effort ratio.
Can I use my AIOS score in investor materials or sales? +
Yes. Several of our audited products have included their AIOS score and gap register in Series A/B due diligence packages. It demonstrates technical maturity and a structured approach to AI risk — both of which are increasingly important to investors and enterprise buyers. The score is your number to share. We recommend sharing the full report with the gap register rather than just the score, as the roadmap shows forward progress.
